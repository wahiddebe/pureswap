Fund Quality Score Explanation

The score estimates the likelihood that an address or transaction is linked to illegal activities. It ranges from 0% (Low Risk) to 100% (High Risk).
Based on this score, the risk is divided into three categories:

<20%

High Risk

Transfers from this wallet have a very high chance of being blocked by exchanges or other services due to strong ties to illicit activities.

Do not send funds from this wallet to centralized platforms like Binance or Coinbase.

<50%

Mid Risk

There is a moderate chance that transfers from this wallet could be flagged, delayed, or blocked, and you may be asked to provide additional KYC information.

Proceed with caution when sending funds from this wallet and be prepared for potential complications

>80%

Low Risk

Transfers from this wallet are unlikely to be blocked or flagged and should proceed smoothly.

You can safely send funds from this wallet to most platforms without expecting issues.

How are scores calculated?

The score is calculated by analyzing factors that suggest potential involvement in illicit activities. These factors reflect patterns that centralized exchanges (e.g., Binance, Coinbase) and reputable services monitor for compliance. Some key factors include:

High Risk Factors

These indicate strong ties to illicit activities, often triggering immediate flags.

Darknet Market Links:

Direct or indirect transactions with darknet marketplaces involved in illegal goods trading.

Sanctioned Addresses:

Wallets linked to OFAC, UN, or other sanctions lists, indicating potential terrorist financing or prohibited entities.

Mid Risk Factors

These suggest moderate risk, often due to weaker controls or suspicious patterns.

Crypto ATM Usage:

Transactions via crypto ATMs with minimal KYC, vulnerable to cash-based laundering.

P2P Platforms:

Trades on peer-to-peer platforms (e.g., LocalBitcoins) with inconsistent KYC enforcement.

Low Risk Factors

These are associated with compliant, transparent activities, typically on regulated platforms.

Reputable Exchange Transactions:

Trades on licensed exchanges like Binance with strong KYC/AML policies.

Full KYC Compliance:

Wallets linked to verified identities (e.g., passport, address) via robust KYC processes.
